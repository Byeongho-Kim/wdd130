What Is EDM?

EDM is an abbreviation of Electronic Dance Music, and is a generic term for electronic music used in commercial party culture such as clubs and festivals. The chart called Dance has already been individually classified on US Billboard and UK official charts and has been awarded related awards. After 2010, the term EDM was used to re-examine the rave/festival culture in which the American music market and media are reviving. Electronic music is exceptionally diverse in sub-genres and classifications than other genres. This is due to the fact that electronic music has multiple origins and, over time, blends simultaneously with various regional and cultural factors.

How genres are dinstinguished?

The basic classification criterion is the drum beat that is applied to the song, and the frame of a large genre is mostly changed according to the drum beat and BPM, and the detailed genre differs according to various factors such as the sound source used and the music composition format.

Genres below 80 BPM include dubstep, trap, and future bass. Genres between 80 and 120 BPM include house, trance, house, glitch hop, future bass, halftime drum and bass, and drumstep. Between 120 and 170 BPM are trance, house, hardstyle, and garage, and above 170 BPM are hardcore techno, jungle, drum and bass.
